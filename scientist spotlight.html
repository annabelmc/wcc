Claudia Alexander

It's been a fabulous mission for planetary science, and it is hard to see it come to an end.

Claudia Alexander worked at the United States Geological Survey studying plate tectonics and the Ames Research Center observing Jovian moons, before moving to NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory in 1986. She worked as science coordinator for the plasma wave instrument aboard the Galileo spacecraft before becoming the project manager of the Galileo mission. She was the final project manager for the mission, and oversaw the spacecraft's dive into Jupiter's atmosphere at the mission's conclusion in 2003

Interesting Facts

  1. Claudia was born in Santa Clara, California
  2. Claudia was a member of the American Geophysical Union
  3. The European Space Agency's Rosetta mission honored Claudia naming a feature after her on the mission's target, comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko
  4. Claudia was a researcher on diverse topics, including: the evolution and interior physics of comets>
